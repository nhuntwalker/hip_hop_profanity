<p><i><b>D12 World</b></i> is the second <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/Detroit_hip_hop" title="Detroit hip hop" class="mw-redirect">Detroit hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_ensemble" title="Music ensemble" class="mw-redirect">group</a> <a href="/wiki/D12" title="D12">D12</a>, released on April 27, 2004. The album sold 544,000 copies in its first week on the U.S. Billboard 200.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The album is the last D12 album to feature <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a> before his death in 2006, and the second D12 album to feature "Bugz" on a track.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D12_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In memory of <a href="/wiki/Bugz" title="Bugz" class="mw-redirect">Bugz</a>, who was killed prior to a concert on May 21, 1999, the group recorded the track "Good Die Young" in memory of him. The album also includes a song entitled "Bugz '97", which is a 1997 recording of Bugz, originally taken from the song "Desperados". The album was often overshadowed by Eminem's massive success, and as such, the band toured without him for promotion of the album. At the time, Eminem was busy recording <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(Eminem_album)" title="Encore (Eminem album)">Encore</a></i>. Although shadowed by <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s success, members <a href="/wiki/Bizarre_(rapper)" title="Bizarre (rapper)">Bizarre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a> managed to prevail with mildly successful solo careers following <i>D12 World</i>, with the releases of <i><a href="/wiki/Hannicap_Circus" title="Hannicap Circus">Hannicap Circus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Jerry_Garcia" title="Searching for Jerry Garcia">Searching for Jerry Garcia</a></i> in 2005. Before the album's release, "6 in the Morning" was featured on Eminem's 2003 <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Straight_from_the_Lab" title="Straight from the Lab">Straight from the Lab</a></i> and was entitled "Come On In".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D12_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>D12 World</i> debuted at the top of the U.S., U.K., Irish, Canadian and NZ album charts. It debuted at number 2 in Germany and Australia, and also in the top 5 in Switzerland, Norway and Australia. It debuted in the top 10 in Sweden, Denmark, France, Belgium and the Netherlands. It sold over 500,000 records in its first week of release in the United States alone. "<a href="/wiki/My_Band" title="My Band">My Band</a>", the first single, also has been successful reaching number one in Australia and the U.S. rhythmic top 40, top 5 in the U.K. and Germany and top 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>. Upon release, D12 World received mixed to positive reviews from critics. At Metacritic, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an average score of 58, based on 13 reviews, which indicates "mixed or average reviews."<sup id="cite_ref-Meta_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meta-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p><b>Notes</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The skit "Bugz" is taken from the song "Desperados", performed by Eminem, Proof, Bugz &amp; The Almighty Dreadknaughts.</li>
<li>The song "Just Like U" doesn't appear on the clean version of the album.</li>
<li>The song "Barbershop" can be found on the <a href="/wiki/Barbershop_2:_Back_in_Business" title="Barbershop 2: Back in Business">Barbershop 2: Back in Business</a> soundtrack.</li>
<li>The song "Just Like U" contains a sample from "Sir Galahad" by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Wakeman" title="Rick Wakeman">Rick Wakeman</a>.</li>
</ul>
