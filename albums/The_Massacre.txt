<p><i><b>The Massacre</b></i> is the second <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, released March 3, 2005 on <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> in the United States. Its initially planned release was pushed five days ahead to avoid <a href="/wiki/Internet_leak" title="Internet leak">Internet leakage</a>. The album debuted at number one on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, selling over 1.14 million copies in its first week. Upon its release, <i>The Massacre</i> received generally positive reviews from most music critics. It was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Grammy Award for Best Rap Album</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> losing to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Registration" title="Late Registration">Late Registration</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/48th_Grammy_Awards" title="48th Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">48th Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Massacre</i> has a music video for every track on the special edition version of the album. The original title for the album was <i><a href="/wiki/St._Valentine%27s_Day_Massacre" title="St. Valentine's Day Massacre" class="mw-redirect">St. Valentine's Day Massacre</a></i> and was arranged to be released on February 14, 2005, but was postponed and the album's title was shortened to <i>The Massacre</i>. The censored version of the album censors out most profanity, violence, and all drug content. "Gunz Come Out" has inconsistency in the editing, and contains some profanity. In comparison, the album is not as heavily censored as <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(album)" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin' (album)">Get Rich or Die Tryin'</a></i>, but it is still a very highly censored album ranking in severity with albums such as <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Thoughts_of_a_Predicate_Felon" title="Thoughts of a Predicate Felon">Thoughts of a Predicate Felon</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Stillmatic" title="Stillmatic">Stillmatic</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Massacre</i> sold 1.14 million copies in its first week of release, making it the seventh highest-selling album since <a href="/wiki/SoundScan" title="SoundScan" class="mw-redirect">SoundScan</a> began tracking sales data in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the third best opening week for any hip hop album in history, behind <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP" title="The Marshall Mathers LP">The Marshall Mathers LP</a></i> which sold over 1.79 million copies in its first week and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eminem_Show" title="The Eminem Show">The Eminem Show</a></i> which sold 1.32 million copies for its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p><i>The Massacre</i> received generally positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>; it holds a score of 66 out of 100 at <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a></i> magazine found it "full of finger-pointing panache" and wrote that "50 delivers a taut, albeit less explosive, album aimed at both silencing his detractors and keeping the ladies satisfied".<sup id="cite_ref-Rodriguez_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> observed "a new depth to the murderous lyricism" from 50 Cent on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-NME_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NME-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Greg_Tate" title="Greg Tate">Greg Tate</a>, writing in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i>, said that, like <a href="/wiki/Tupac" title="Tupac">Tupac</a>, 50 Cent is "a ruffian who knows the value of a good pop <a href="/wiki/Hook_(music)" title="Hook (music)">hook</a>", and called <i>The Massacre</i> "the most diabolically sensous collection of baby-making gangsta music since Pac's <i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_on_Me" title="All Eyez on Me">All Eyez</a></i>."<sup id="cite_ref-Tate_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tate-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Kelefa Sanneh of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> found the album to be "nearly as addictive as its predecessor" and called 50 Cent "a crafty songwriter, specializing in obvious but nearly irresistible tracks that sound better the more you hear them."<sup id="cite_ref-Sanneh_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sanneh-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> In his review for <i>The Village Voice</i>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> said that 50 Cent's "ugly gangsta lies" are "incidental to the mood of the piece, which is friendly, relaxed, good-humored, and in the groove."<sup id="cite_ref-Christgau_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christgau-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In a mixed review, <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Rabin" title="Nathan Rabin">Nathan Rabin</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> said that, although its strengths lie in 50 Cent's "dark charisma" and "fluid delivery", the album is marred by flaws typical of "big rap releases: At nearly 78 minutes, it's far too long, wildly uneven, and not particularly cohesive sonically or thematically."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Uncut_(magazine)" title="Uncut (magazine)">Uncut</a></i> magazine wrote that, despite 50 Cent's "cool menace", "not even tight productions from Eminem and Dre can stop things from flagging midway."<sup id="cite_ref-Uncut_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Uncut-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Lynne d Johnson of <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> felt that it lacks "originality" and makes artistic concessions: "He's tryin' too hard to be everything to everybody."<sup id="cite_ref-Johnson_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Johnson-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> In a negative review for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Petridis" title="Alexis Petridis">Alexis Petridis</a> panned him as a lyricist and felt that the album lacks "any of the factors that make the best gangsta rap disturbingly compelling ... There's nothing except a string of cliches so limited that repetition is unavoidable".<sup id="cite_ref-Petridis_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Petridis-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Massacre</i> was nominated at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Grammy_Awards" title="2006 Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">2006 Grammy Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Album" title="Best Rap Album" class="mw-redirect">Best Rap Album</a>, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Registration" title="Late Registration">Late Registration</a></i>. It was ranked the tenth best album of the year by <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstone2005_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone2005-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_2">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Massacre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The album was re-released under the name <i>The Massacre (Special Edition)</i>. It was re-released on September 6, 2005 with a <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of "Outta Control" featuring <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>. The re-release included a bonus DVD with <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> for all of the songs (except for Disco Inferno, Gunz Come Out &amp; the Intro), and the <a href="/wiki/Trailer_(film)" title="Trailer (film)" class="mw-redirect">trailer</a> for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(2005_film)" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin' (2005 film)" class="mw-redirect">Get Rich or Die Tryin'</a></i> and excludes track 22, the <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> <a href="/wiki/Hate_It_or_Love_It_(remix)" title="Hate It or Love It (remix)" class="mw-redirect">remix</a> of "<a href="/wiki/Hate_It_or_Love_It" title="Hate It or Love It">Hate It or Love It</a>", clearly due to the ongoing feud between 50 Cent and <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a> and just leaves 21 songs. The re-release helped it re-climb to number two in the United States.</p>
