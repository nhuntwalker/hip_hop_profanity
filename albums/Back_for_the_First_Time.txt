<p><i><b>Back for the First Time</b></i> is the second <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The album, which serves as his <a href="/wiki/Major_record_label" title="Major record label" class="mw-redirect">major label</a> debut, was released on October 17, 2000, under <a href="/wiki/Disturbing_tha_Peace" title="Disturbing tha Peace">Disturbing tha Peace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Back_for_the_First_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of the tracks are taken from his first studio album, the independently released <i><a href="/wiki/Incognegro" title="Incognegro">Incognegro</a></i> (1999), except for "Stick 'Em Up", "Southern Hospitality", a <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of "<a href="/wiki/What%27s_Your_Fantasy" title="What's Your Fantasy">What's Your Fantasy</a>", and "Phat Rabbit". The album debuted at number four US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, and sold 133,000 copies in its first-week of sales.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>The album has since been certified 3x platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a>, with over 3.1 million copies sold.</p>
