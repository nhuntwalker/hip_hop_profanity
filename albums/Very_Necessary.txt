<p><i><b>Very Necessary</b></i> the fourth studio album by rap/hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Salt-n-Pepa" title="Salt-n-Pepa">Salt-n-Pepa</a> Released in October 12,<a href="/wiki/1993_in_music" title="1993 in music">1993</a>. The album went on to become the most successful rap album by a female act. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Cheryl_James" title="Cheryl James">Cheryl James</a> (Salt), <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Denton" title="Sandra Denton">Sandy Denton</a> (Pepa), <a href="/wiki/DJ_Spinderella" title="DJ Spinderella">Dee Dee "Diedra" Roper</a> (Spinderella), and <a href="/wiki/Hurby_Azor" title="Hurby Azor">Hurby Azor</a>. It resulted in several <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> hits for the female hip-hop trio, including "<a href="/wiki/Shoop_(song)" title="Shoop (song)">Shoop</a>" (their first top 5 pop hit, peaking at #4), "<a href="/wiki/Whatta_Man" title="Whatta Man" class="mw-redirect">Whatta Man</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/En_Vogue" title="En Vogue">En Vogue</a>, their biggest hit at #3), and "None of Your Business", a top 40 hit which would win them their first <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>. <i>Very Necessary</i> peaked at #4 on the US Billboard Top 200 Albums Chart, and has been certified 5 times platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> for sales in excess of five million copies in the US. The album sold an additional two million copies internationally, for total sales of seven million.</p>
