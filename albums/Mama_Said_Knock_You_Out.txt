<p><i><b>Mama Said Knock You Out</b></i> is the fourth <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> artist <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>. The album was released in 1990, after the critically disappointing <i><a href="/wiki/Walking_with_a_Panther" title="Walking with a Panther">Walking with a Panther</a></i> in 1989. <i>Mama Said Knock You Out</i> is now seen as a classic hip-hop album.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama_Said_Knock_You_Out&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album spawned four hit singles: "The Boomin' System," "Around the Way Girl," (which sampled clips from the <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jane_Girls" title="Mary Jane Girls">Mary Jane Girls</a> song "All Night Long"), the hard-hitting title track itself (which received special notice after LL Cool J's dynamic <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation_(music)" title="Instrumentation (music)">full-live-band</a> performance of it - unusual for rap performers/performances at the time - during an episode of <i>MTV's Unplugged</i>), and "6 Minutes Of Pleasure." The <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" title="Diss track">diss record</a> "To Da Break Of Dawn" was named number 11 on <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i></a>'s 20 greatest diss records of all time. The album reached No. 16 in the U.S. charts. The album eventually went 2X Platinum selling over two million copies according to the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The title song reached number 17<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> and was certified gold by the RIAA. LL Cool J won <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Solo_Performance" title="Best Rap Solo Performance" class="mw-redirect">Best Rap Solo Performance</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_1992" title="Grammy Awards of 1992" class="mw-redirect">Grammy Awards of 1992</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama_Said_Knock_You_Out&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"This 22-year-old veteran has lost neither his eye for everyday detail nor his sheer relish for words…" observed Mark Cooper in a 3/5 review for <i><a href="/wiki/Q_(magazine)" title="Q (magazine)" class="mw-redirect">Q</a></i>. "If LL frequently sounds like he's torn between stardom, egomania and his insistence that he's still the boy next door, 'Around the Way Girl' and jams like 'The Boomin' System' suggest that there's plenty of mileage left in this particular balancing act."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> "LL's stack of samples add the icing to a cake that is all dark, remorseless rhythm, a lo-fi drum beat shadowed by a crude bass rumble," noted Richard Cook in a 4/5 review for <i><a href="/wiki/Select_(magazine)" title="Select (magazine)">Select</a></i>. "It could be <a href="/wiki/Dub_(music)" title="Dub (music)">Jamaican dub</a> they're making here, if it weren't for LL's slipper lip."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album was included in <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Connection" title="Hip Hop Connection">Hip Hop Connection</a>'</i>s <i>The phat forty</i>, a rundown of rap's greatest albums. "The LP's title track proved to be the single of the year and probably LL's best record since '<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Bad" title="I'm Bad">I'm Bad</a>'," <i>HHC</i> said, "while 'Eat 'Em Up L Chill' and 'To Da Break Of Dawn' was [sic] the sound of Cool J getting his own back – and in style."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1998, it was listed in The Source's 100 Best Rap Albums. In 2005, comedian <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a> listed it as the sixth greatest hip-hop album ever in a guest article for <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampling">Sampling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama_Said_Knock_You_Out&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sampling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>"The Boomin' System" samples from <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Payback" title="The Payback">The Payback</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Funky_Drummer" title="Funky Drummer">Funky Drummer</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" title="Public Enemy (band)" class="mw-redirect">Public Enemy</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Bring_the_Noise" title="Bring the Noise">Bring the Noise</a>"</li>
<li>"Around the Way Girl" samples from <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jane_Girls" title="Mary Jane Girls">Mary Jane Girls</a>' "All Night Long," Keni Burke's "Risin' to the Top," and the Honey Drippers' "Impeach The President."</li>
<li>"Eat 'em Up, L Chill" samples from <a href="/wiki/George_Clinton_(funk_musician)" title="George Clinton (funk musician)" class="mw-redirect">George Clinton</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Atomic_Dog" title="Atomic Dog">Atomic Dog</a>" and the Five Stairsteps' "Don't Change Your Love."</li>
<li>"Mr. Good Bar" samples from All The People's "Cramp Your Style," <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>'s "Get Up, Get Into It, Get Involved," and <a href="/wiki/ESG_(band)" title="ESG (band)">ESG</a>'s "UFO."</li>
<li>"Murdergram (Live at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapmania&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rapmania (page does not exist)">Rapmania</a>)" samples from James Brown's "My Thang," <a href="/wiki/Earth,_Wind_%26_Fire" title="Earth, Wind &amp; Fire">Earth, Wind &amp; Fire</a>'s "Moment of Truth," and ESG's "UFO."</li>
<li>"Cheesy Rat Blues" samples <a href="/w/index.php?title=Twin_Hype&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Twin Hype (page does not exist)">Twin Hype</a>'s "Nothin' Could Save Ya" and <a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>'s "Vapors".</li>
<li>"Farmers Blvd. (Our Anthem)" samples <a href="/wiki/Willie_Hutch" title="Willie Hutch">Willie Hutch</a>'s "Brothers Gonna Work it Out" and "Mack Man," <a href="/wiki/Barry_White" title="Barry White">Barry White</a>'s "I'm Gonna Love You Just A Little More, Babe," and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Preston" title="Billy Preston">Billy Preston</a>'s "I Wrote A Simple Song,".</li>
<li>"Mama Said Knock You Out" samples <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Funky_Drummer" title="Funky Drummer">Funky Drummer</a>," the Chicago Gangsters' "Gangster Boogie," <a href="/wiki/Sly_%26_The_Family_Stone" title="Sly &amp; The Family Stone" class="mw-redirect">Sly &amp; The Family Stone</a>'s "Trip to Your Heart" and "<a href="/wiki/Sing_a_Simple_Song" title="Sing a Simple Song">Sing a Simple Song</a>", and <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Bells_(song)" title="Rock the Bells (song)">Rock the Bells</a> by LL Cool J.</li>
<li>"To da Break of Dawn" samples James Brown's "<a href="/wiki/Funky_President" title="Funky President" class="mw-redirect">Funky President</a>," <a href="/wiki/Maceo_%26_All_the_King%27s_Men" title="Maceo &amp; All the King's Men" class="mw-redirect">Maceo &amp; All the King's Men's</a> "Got to Getcha," and <a href="/wiki/Pleasure" title="Pleasure">Pleasure</a>'s "Joyous."</li>
<li>"6 Minutes of Pleasure" samples James Brown's "Funky President" and Doug E. Fresh's "<a href="/wiki/The_Show_(Doug_E._Fresh_song)" title="The Show (Doug E. Fresh song)">The Show</a>."</li>
<li>"Illegal Search" samples from James Brown's "Mind Power" and <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Thomas" title="Rufus Thomas">Rufus Thomas</a>'s "The Breakdown Pt. I &amp; II."</li>
</ul>
