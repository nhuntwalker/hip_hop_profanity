<p><i><b>E. 1999 Eternal</b></i> is the second <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-N-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-N-Harmony" class="mw-redirect">Bone Thugs-N-Harmony</a>, released July 25, 1995, on <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>. The album was released four months after the death of rapper <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>, the group's mentor and the executive producer of the album. Both the album and single "<a href="/wiki/Tha_Crossroads" title="Tha Crossroads">Tha Crossroads</a>" are dedicated to him. Following up on the surprise success of their breakthrough single "<a href="/wiki/Thuggish_Ruggish_Bone" title="Thuggish Ruggish Bone">Thuggish Ruggish Bone</a>", it became a popular album and received positive reviews from music critics, earning praise for the group's melodic <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> style. The album title is a portmentau of the then-future year 1999 and Cleveland's eastside neighborhood centering around East 99th Street and St. Clair Avenue where the group is based from.</p>
<p><i>E. 1999 Eternal</i> became the group's best-selling album, with over five million copies sold in the United States and ten million worldwide. It topped the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> for two consecutive weeks. The album was nominated for the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Grammy Award for Best Rap Album</a>, ultimately losing to <a href="/wiki/Naughty_by_Nature" title="Naughty by Nature">Naughty by Nature</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Poverty%27s_Paradise" title="Poverty's Paradise">Poverty's Paradise</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Grammy_Awards" title="1996 Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">1996 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Critical reviews of <i>E. 1999 Eternal</i> were mostly positive and has been hailed by many fans as a hip-hop classic. Jason Birchmeier of <a href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic" class="mw-redirect">Allmusic</a> described it as "an impressive debut full-length that dismisses any notion that the group was merely a one-hit wonder" and claims that it "maintains a consistent tone, one that's menacing and somber, produced entirely by <a href="/wiki/DJ_U-Neek" title="DJ U-Neek">DJ U-Neek</a>, a Los Angeles-based producer who frames the songs with dark, smoked-out G-funk beats and synth melodies."<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
