<p><i><b>Harlem World</b></i> is the critically acclaimed multi-platinum debut album by <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase</a>, released October 28, 1997. The album was nominated at the <a href="/wiki/41st_Grammy_Awards" title="41st Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">41st Grammy Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Grammy Award for Best Rap Album</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harlem_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Seen as <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a>'s new protégé since the passing of <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, Mase rose to fame after being a member of <a href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Corn_(group)" title="Children of the Corn (group)">Children of the Corn</a> with fellow Harlem rappers <a href="/wiki/Big_L_(rapper)" title="Big L (rapper)" class="mw-redirect">Big L</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam'ron">Cam'ron</a>. After the death of Bloodshed and the subsequent disbanding of the group, Mase was signed to <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy</a>, catching the public eye when appearing on the track "Mo Money, Mo Problems" from Biggie's 1997 album <i><a href="/wiki/Life_After_Death" title="Life After Death">Life After Death</a></i>. Puff Daddy began to promote Mase as the new premier act of Bad Boy and within a year had released "Harlem World", a successful album spawning hits such as "Feel So Good" (U.S. #5), "What You Want" (U.S. #6), and "Lookin' at Me" (U.S. #8).</p>
<p>Mase released his first solo LP, <i>Harlem World</i>, which debuted at #1 on the Billboard Pop and R&amp;B LP charts selling over 270,000 copies in the U.S. its first week of release; it has since gone on to sell 3.3 million copies in the United States. Joining Mase on his solo LP was a long list of rap elite including <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">The Lox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil' Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Total_(band)" title="Total (band)" class="mw-redirect">Total</a>, and his close friend <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>. The album featured production by Combs, <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deric_Angelettie" title="Deric Angelettie" class="mw-redirect">Deric "D-Dot" Angelettie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_J" title="Stevie J">Steven "Stevie J" Jordan</a> and (then unknowns) <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a>. Mase told MTV about his first solo effort: "Well, basically what I'm trying to establish is a strong identity and foundation for Mase so a lot of people could know that Mase is his own person and Mase can do other things besides rap and music and things in that nature."</p>

<p><i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> said of the album: "...creatively refreshing, well-crafted lyrics... rap's newest bad boy more than holds his own on his solo debut... his distinctive marble-mouthed drawl... creates a regular-guy persona all too rare in hip-hop."Muzik (4/9, p.84) - 4 stars (out of 5) - "...Harlem on the rise? Most definitely." Rap Pages (1/98, p.105) - Solid Material - "...Creatively refreshing, well-crafted lyrics and a high-energy track over a hyped beat easily make this..."</p>
<p>In 2008, the single "Feel So Good" was ranked number 67 on VH1's 100 Greatest Songs of Hip Hop.</p>
