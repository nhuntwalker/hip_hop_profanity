<p><i><b>Beg for Mercy</b></i> is the debut album by <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">group</a> <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, at the time composed of rappers <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>, with support from <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a>. The album was released in November 2003, 9 months after 50 Cent's successful debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(album)" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin' (album)">Get Rich or Die Tryin'</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beg_for_Mercy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"I've been promoting G Unit since before I even had a record deal", says 50. "All the music I put on the mixtape circuit was 50 Cent and G Unit."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As the album was recorded, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a> was sentenced to jail on charges of gun possession, and so he makes only two appearances on the entire record, on the tracks "Groupie Love" and "I Smell Pussy". His image is seen on the brick wall of the album cover as he could not be photographed due to the jail sentence.</p>
<p>At the time of the album's release, rapper <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" title="Game (rapper)" class="mw-redirect">The Game</a> was just placed in G-Unit, therefore he is not photographed, nor does he make an appearance on the album. He does however make cameos in the videos for singles <a href="/wiki/Stunt_101" title="Stunt 101">Stunt 101</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wanna_Get_To_Know_You" title="Wanna Get To Know You" class="mw-redirect">Wanna Get To Know You</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Poppin%27_Them_Thangs" title="Poppin' Them Thangs">Poppin' Them Thangs</a>. He is also mentioned in Poppin' Them Thangs when <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a> raps, "You ain't no <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crip</a> like <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop</a>, / You ain't no <a href="/wiki/Bloods" title="Bloods">Blood</a> like <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">Game</a>."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales_and_certifications">Sales and certifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beg_for_Mercy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sales and certifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In its first week of sales,'<i>Beg for Mercy</i> sold 377,000 copies. It debuted at #3 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a>, behind <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s eighth posthumous release, <i><a href="/wiki/Tupac:_Resurrection_(soundtrack)" title="Tupac: Resurrection (soundtrack)">Tupac: Resurrection</a></i>, (430,000 units moved), and Jay-Z's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Album_(Jay-Z_album)" title="The Black Album (Jay-Z album)">The Black Album</a></i>, (463,263 units moved.) Its bow was moved up from November 18 to thwart piracy, as was <i>Beg for Mercy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Beg for Mercy</i> then sold 327,000 copies in its second week to peak at #2 on the Billboard 200,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> while moving another 193,000 units in its third week.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The album has now sold over 2 million units in the U.S.,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> 3 million copies worldwide,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> and has been certified 2× <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
