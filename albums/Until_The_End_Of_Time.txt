<p><i><b>Until the End of Time</b></i> is the third posthumous <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> released from the late <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>. This album consists of a collection of unreleased material and <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> end songs from Tupac's "<a href="/wiki/Makaveli" title="Makaveli" class="mw-redirect">Makaveli</a>" period while signed to <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a>. This album was the second album released without Shakur's creative input. <i>Until the End of Time</i> was very highly anticipated and ended up being the best selling hip hop album in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> As of 2011, the album has been <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified</a> triple <a href="/wiki/Platinum" title="Platinum">platinum</a> in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The single "Until the End of Time" features Richard Page and contains a sample from the song "<a href="/wiki/Broken_Wings_(Mr._Mister_song)" title="Broken Wings (Mr. Mister song)">Broken Wings</a>", a 1985 song by <a href="/wiki/Mr._Mister" title="Mr. Mister">Mr. Mister</a> (from which Page is lead singer). The video for this song featured a compilation of unreleased footage of Tupac Shakur. <a href="/wiki/Suge_Knight" title="Suge Knight">Suge Knight</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afeni_Shakur" title="Afeni Shakur">Afeni Shakur</a> were the executive producers of this album, with ten of the songs being produced by longtime Tupac producer and friend <a href="/wiki/Johnny_%22J%22" title="Johnny &quot;J&quot;">Johnny "J"</a>. Most of the tracks were remixed from their original master tape versions and were censored of any references to Death Row Records or its artists (for example, the track "This Ain't Livin" has a shout out to <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, but due to him being a Death Row artist, it was censored to prevent Amaru's association with the label, although this was a joint release between the two labels). Songs like "Fuckin' with the Wrong Nigga", "Ballad of a Dead Soulja", "Good Life", "LastOnesLeft", "Runnin' On E" are the only tracks known to have been kept true to the original mixes, albeit "Ballad of a Dead Soulja" is missing a couple of instruments to keep from registering the original C. Mayfield sample.</p>
<p><i>Until the End of Time</i> debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 with first week sales of 427,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It has sold 2,220,589 copies in the United States as of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
