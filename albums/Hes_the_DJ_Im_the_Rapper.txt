<p><i><b>He's the DJ, I'm the Rapper</b></i> is the second studio album by hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/DJ_Jazzy_Jeff_%26_The_Fresh_Prince" title="DJ Jazzy Jeff &amp; The Fresh Prince">DJ Jazzy Jeff &amp; The Fresh Prince</a>. It was the first <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double album</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop music</a>, in its original vinyl incarnation.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) certified the album triple <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">platinum</a> on February 1, 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1998, the album was selected as one of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i> magazine's 100 Best Rap Albums.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=He%27s_the_DJ,_I%27m_the_Rapper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/Brand_New_Funk" title="Brand New Funk" class="mw-redirect">Brand New Funk</a>", was only released promotionally and, thus, failed to achieve any commercial success. However, the album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Parents_Just_Don%27t_Understand" title="Parents Just Don't Understand">Parents Just Don't Understand</a>", won the first-ever <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance">Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance</a> and reached #12 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Although the album's third single, "<a href="/wiki/Nightmare_on_My_Street" title="Nightmare on My Street" class="mw-redirect">Nightmare on My Street</a>", which reached #15 on the Billboard Hot 100, was considered for inclusion in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/A_Nightmare_on_Elm_Street_4:_The_Dream_Master" title="A Nightmare on Elm Street 4: The Dream Master">A Nightmare on Elm Street 4: The Dream Master</a></i>, the producers of the film decided against its inclusion. As a result, vinyl pressings of the album contain a disclaimer sticker that says, "[This song] is not part of the soundtrack...and is not authorized, licensed, or affiliated with the <i>Nightmare on Elm Street</i> films."</p>
