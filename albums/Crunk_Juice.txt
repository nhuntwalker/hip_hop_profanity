<p><i><b>Crunk Juice</b></i> is the fifth <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America" class="mw-redirect">American</a> recording artists <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon_%26_the_East_Side_Boyz" title="Lil Jon &amp; the East Side Boyz" class="mw-redirect">Lil Jon &amp; the East Side Boyz</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> released in November 16, 2004, on <a href="/wiki/TVT_Records" title="TVT Records">TVT</a>/<a href="/wiki/BME_Recordings" title="BME Recordings">BME</a>. The production was handled by own Lil Jon, who also collaborated in the executive production, alongside Bryan Leach, Rob McDowell, Emperor Searcy, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Phillips" title="Vince Phillips">Vince Phillips</a>, and production by Lil Jay of <a href="/wiki/Crime_Mob" title="Crime Mob">Crime Mob</a>, DJ Flexx, Scotty Beats, <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chad_Hugo" title="Chad Hugo">Hugo</a>, and Rick Rubin. The album contains numerous collaborations from rappers and singers, like <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a> from <a href="/wiki/Underground_Kingz" title="Underground Kingz">UGK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ying_Yang_Twins" title="Ying Yang Twins">Ying Yang Twins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell</a> among others. There is a <a href="/wiki/Chopped_and_screwed" title="Chopped and screwed">chopped and screwed</a> version of the album done by DJ Michael "5000" Watts.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Lil Jon's next solo album, 2010's <a href="/wiki/Crunk_Rock" title="Crunk Rock">Crunk Rock</a>, did not feature the East Side Boyz.</p>
<p>The Album was Certified 2x Platinum By the RIAA in 2005 for Sales of over 2 Million Copies.</p>
