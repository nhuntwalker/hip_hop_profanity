<p><i><b>Pain Is Love</b></i> is the third <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">Rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> released in 2001. Its sequel was released on February 28, 2012, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Pain_Is_Love_2" title="Pain Is Love 2">Pain Is Love 2</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pain_Is_Love&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Pain is Love</i> was commercially successful in the hip hop music scene, it spawned two number one singles, debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 with 361,000 copies sold in the first week and eventually was certified triple platinum in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> It had also received at <a href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy" class="mw-redirect">Grammy</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Album" title="Best Rap Album" class="mw-redirect">Best Rap Album</a> in 2002.</p>
