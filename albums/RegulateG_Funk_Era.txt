<p><i><b>Regulate... G Funk Era</b></i> is the debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a>. It was released in June 7, 1994 by <a href="/wiki/Violator_Records" title="Violator Records" class="mw-redirect">Violator Records</a> and distributed by the <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a> record label. It sold three million copies in the US, reaching <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">triple platinum</a> status. The album's biggest hit was the eponymous single "<a href="/wiki/Regulate_(song)" title="Regulate (song)">Regulate</a>", a gritty depiction of <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">West Coast</a> gang life which samples singer <a href="/wiki/Michael_McDonald_(singer)" title="Michael McDonald (singer)">Michael McDonald</a>'s hit "<a href="/wiki/I_Keep_Forgettin%27_(Every_Time_You%27re_Near)" title="I Keep Forgettin' (Every Time You're Near)">I Keep Forgettin' (Every Time You're Near)</a>" and featured <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>. The song Regulate was also featured on the <a href="/wiki/Above_the_Rim" title="Above the Rim">Above the Rim</a> <a href="/wiki/Above_the_Rim_(soundtrack)" title="Above the Rim (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a>, which was released on March 22, 1994. The album also contained the top ten hit "<a href="/wiki/This_D.J." title="This D.J.">This D.J.</a>"</p>

<p>The album debuted at #2 on the US Billboard Top 200 Album Chart selling 176,000 its opening week. Warren G had two Grammy nominations: "This D.J." was nominated for a 1995 Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance, while "Regulate" was nominated for a 1995 Grammy for Best Rap Performance By A Duo Or Group. The album received some positive reviews from critics, with <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> commenting positively regarding the coolly menacing nature of the music.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> (8/11/94, p.69) - 3 Stars - Good - "...fat-bottomed, mellowed-out sound that conjures up the days of way back. Its lazy, loping shuffle suits the Southern California climate..."</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> (9/94, p.135) - Highly Recommended - "Truth be told, Warren G. wasn't cut out to be a hardass. He's a romantic, in love with soft sound."</p>
