<p><i><b>The Blueprint<sup>2</sup>: The Gift &amp; the Curse</b></i> is <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s seventh <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a>, released November 12, 2002. Parts of the album were later reissued as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blueprint_2.1" title="The Blueprint 2.1" class="mw-redirect">The Blueprint 2.1</a></i> in 2003.</p>
<p>This album, like Jay-Z's previous four, debuted at #1 with over 545,000 units shipped in its first week of sales, and has sold 2,117,000 units as of February 2012 in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The album was criticized for its more pop-oriented sound as well as its plethora of guests, while <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blueprint" title="The Blueprint">The Blueprint</a></i> had been almost devoid of guest appearances.</p>
<p>Though the album has no strict concept in view, the first disc <i>The Gift</i> generally has more upbeat, party tracks, including the hit singles "<a href="/wiki/%2703_Bonnie_%26_Clyde" title="'03 Bonnie &amp; Clyde">'03 Bonnie &amp; Clyde</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Excuse_Me_Miss" title="Excuse Me Miss">Excuse Me Miss</a>." <i>The Curse</i> has a somewhat darker tone, including fewer guest appearances, disses to <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, and Jay-Z's former mentor <a href="/wiki/Jaz-O" title="Jaz-O">Jaz-O</a> on "Blueprint²" and a dramatic street tale on "Meet the Parents."</p>
