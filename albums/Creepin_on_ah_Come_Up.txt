<p><i><b>Creepin on ah Come Up</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> by rap group <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-N-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-N-Harmony" class="mw-redirect">Bone Thugs-N-Harmony</a>. The album was released on June 21, 1994 on <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>. In 1998, the album was selected as one of The Source's 100 Best Rap Albums.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Creepin_on_ah_Come_Up&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>'s first album, with the singles "<a href="/wiki/Thuggish_Ruggish_Bone" title="Thuggish Ruggish Bone">Thuggish Ruggish Bone</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Foe_tha_Love_of_$" title="Foe tha Love of $">Foe tha Love of $</a>". Features on the album include Shatasha Williams (the first <a href="/wiki/Mo_Thugs" title="Mo Thugs">Mo Thugs</a> member) and their mentor and executive producer <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>, and these collaborations began a new fad of having sung vocals for choruses and tight flowing lyrics. The first two lines of "Intro" are backwards. Played forward are "Heaven in art which Father our, Our Father which art in Heaven" Tracks 3, 4 and 6 have listed, "Keenu Songs" which is "U-Neek" spelled backwards. In The Source (8-97) article "Crossroads To Riches" Bone states that they changed their name to Bone Thugs-n-Harmony because they had a song called "Thugs-N-Harmony".</p>
