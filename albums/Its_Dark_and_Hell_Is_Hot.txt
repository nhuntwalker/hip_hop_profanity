<p><i><b>It's Dark and Hell is Hot</b></i> is the debut album of American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, released May 12, 1998 on <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The album featured four singles with music videos, "<a href="/wiki/Get_at_Me_Dog" title="Get at Me Dog">Get at Me Dog</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Stop_Being_Greedy" title="Stop Being Greedy">Stop Being Greedy</a>", "<a href="/wiki/How%27s_It_Goin%27_Down" title="How's It Goin' Down">How's It Goin' Down</a>" and the "<a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Anthem" title="Ruff Ryders Anthem" class="mw-redirect">Ruff Ryders Anthem</a>". The album is widely considered a classic by numerous hip hop fans and critics.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=It%27s_Dark_and_Hell_Is_Hot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=It%27s_Dark_and_Hell_Is_Hot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the album was released, DMX collaborated with producers <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a>, P.K., <a href="/wiki/Dame_Grease" title="Dame Grease">Dame Grease</a>, and Swizz Beatz. Each producer utilized their particular style while creating the album's instrumentals. <i>It's Dark and Hell Is Hot</i> features Gothic instrumentals created mostly by P.K.; DMX raps mostly about violence. DMX is commonly known for his rough voice; "dog barks" and "dog growls" are featured throughout the album. Many of the songs on It's Dark and Hell Is Hot are made up of dark beats and harsh lyrics, showing anticipation of violence. Compared to aggressive songs like "Intro", tracks like "Ruff Ryders' Anthem", "How's It Goin' Down" have a lighter theme and delivery. The album also features more introspective tracks including "Let Me Fly", "For My Dogs", and others. "Damien" is a Scarface style story, with characters voiced by DMX showing DMX working for a local hot dog seller named Damien (who wishes to break into crime, but just needs DMX as 'muscle'), and makes a deal which involves committing acts of violence in return for money and street cred.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyrical_themes">Lyrical themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=It%27s_Dark_and_Hell_Is_Hot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lyrical themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>It's Dark and Hell Is Hot</i> is known for featuring very extreme lyrics about violence with songs like "Intro","X Is Coming", and a lot more. The album is still known for DMX's painful past life when he was younger which is known on "Look Thru My Eyes" and "Let Me Fly". More topics are included like love, <a href="/wiki/Rage_(emotion)" title="Rage (emotion)">rage</a>, stories, and <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>. Unlike many hip hop albums DMX features <a href="/wiki/Prayers" title="Prayers" class="mw-redirect">prayers</a> which are dialogues with him and God.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> critic, Miles Marshall Lewis, talked about the lyrics in "X-Is Coming" and he quoted these lines from the song: "If you got a daughter older than fifteen, I'm a rape her/Take her on the living-room floor, right there in fronta you/Then ask you seriously, 'What you wanna do?'" In his response for his reaction to the extreme violent writing ability of DMX, he answered: "Even within the context of a hardcore rap album, DMX goes beyond the pale."<sup id="cite_ref-rs_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rs-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Some of DMX's most-violent lyrics, inspired by horror, crime, and hardcore rap created a style that would dominate a portion of the rap industry. The earliest examples of these lyrics would be the unreleased songs "Gotti Style" (Featuring Ja Rule), "Read About It" (Featuring Ja Rule), many unreleased freestyles, and his verse on <a href="/wiki/Mic_Geronimo" title="Mic Geronimo">Mic Geronimo</a>'s 1995 song "Time To Build" with appearances by an early <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>.</p>
