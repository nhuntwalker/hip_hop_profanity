<p><i><b>All Eyez on Me</b></i> is the fifth <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2Pac</a>, released February 13, 1996 under <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a> and distributed by <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.</p>
<p>The album is frequently recognized as one of the crowning achievements of 1990s rap music.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> AMG stated that "despite some undeniable filler, it is easily the best production 2Pac's ever had on record".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> It was <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified</a> 5× Platinum after two months in April 1996 and was later certified 9x Platinum in 1998. The album featured the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> number one singles "<a href="/wiki/How_Do_U_Want_It" title="How Do U Want It">How Do U Want It</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/California_Love" title="California Love">California Love</a>". It featured five singles in all, the most of any of Shakur's album. Moreover, <i>All Eyez on Me</i> (which was the only Death Row release to be distributed through <a href="/wiki/PolyGram" title="PolyGram">PolyGram</a> by way of <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a>) made history as the first double-full-length hip-hop solo studio album released for mass consumption. It was issued on two compact discs and four LPs. Chart-wise, <i>All Eyez on Me</i> was the second album from 2Pac to hit number-one on both the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> charts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> It sold 566,000 copies in the first week of its release, and was charted on the top 100 with the top one-week SoundScan sales since 1991. It has sold 5,887,630 copies in the US as of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Tupac_Month:_2Pac.E2.80.99s_Discography_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tupac_Month:_2Pac.E2.80.99s_Discography-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album won the 1997 Soul Train R&amp;B/Soul or Rap Album of the Year Award.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Shakur also won the Award for Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop Artist at the 24th Annual American Music Awards and is also commonly referred to as one of the greatest hip hop/rap albums of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recording">Recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Eyez_on_Me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 1995, <a href="/wiki/Suge_Knight" title="Suge Knight">Suge Knight</a> and&#160;<a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Iovine" title="Jimmy Iovine">Jimmy Iovine</a> paid the $1.4 million bail necessary to get Shakur released from jail on charges of sexual abuse. At the time, Shakur was broke and thus unable to make bail himself. <i>All Eyez on Me</i> was released following an agreement between Knight and Shakur which stated Shakur would make three albums under Death Row Records in return for them paying his bail. Fulfilling part of Shakur's brand new contract, this double-album served as the first two albums of his three album contract.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The songs on <i>All Eyez on Me</i> are, in general, unapologetic celebrations of living the "Thug Lifestyle". Though there is the occasional reminiscence about past and present friends, it is a definite move away from the social and political consciousness of <i><a href="/wiki/2Pacalypse_Now" title="2Pacalypse Now">2Pacalypse Now</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_4_My_N.I.G.G.A.Z." title="Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z." class="mw-redirect">Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z.</a></i>.</p>
<p>The album features occasional guest spots from 2Pac's regulars, such as former-<a href="/wiki/Thug_Life" title="Thug Life">Thug Life</a> members and the <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Clinton_(musician)" title="George Clinton (musician)">George Clinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rappin%27_4-Tay" title="Rappin' 4-Tay">Rappin' 4-Tay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a> among others. The song "Heartz of Men" samples a portion of Richard Pryor's comedy album <i><a href="/wiki/That_Nigger%27s_Crazy" title="That Nigger's Crazy">That Nigger's Crazy</a></i>. Most of the album was produced by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_%22J%22" title="Johnny &quot;J&quot;">Johnny "J"</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, with help from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> on the songs "<a href="/wiki/California_Love" title="California Love">California Love</a>", which he himself appeared in also as an album guest spot, and "Can't C Me", which was Clinton's appearance.</p>
<p><i>All Eyez On Me</i> is 2Pac's best selling album.<sup id="cite_ref-Tupac_Month:_2Pac.E2.80.99s_Discography_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tupac_Month:_2Pac.E2.80.99s_Discography-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> The album was certified 5x Platinum in the United States after just 2 months and was later certified 9x Platinum.<sup id="cite_ref-TupacShakurASearch_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TupacShakurASearch-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> It was re-released in 2001 as enhanced CDs containing the "California Love" music video. Both discs contained the same data track. It was also re-released as a Dual-Disc in 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Eyez_on_Me&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>All Eyez on Me</i> received critical acclaim from a number of sources. The album was included in <i>Rolling Stone</i><span style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s essential recordings of the '90s.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Spin</i> magazine gave it a 10 out of 10, writing that, "As long as you don't expect philanthropy from Tupac, you'll find honesty and some pleasurably twisted scenarios."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> The record ranked No. 3 on <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i><span style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s list of Top 10 albums of 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> A number of other sources celebrated or reacted to the album.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
