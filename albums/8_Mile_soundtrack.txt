<p><i><b>Music from and Inspired by the Motion Picture 8 Mile</b></i> is the official <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> to the 2002 movie <i><a href="/wiki/8_Mile_(film)" title="8 Mile (film)">8 Mile</a></i>. The album, performed by various artists, was released under Shady Records. It spawned the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Lose_Yourself" title="Lose Yourself">Lose Yourself</a>" by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, who also stars in the semi-autobiographical movie. The album also spawned a follow up soundtrack, <i><b>More Music from 8 Mile</b></i>, consisting of songs that appear in the film and were released as singles during the film's time setting of 1995. One of the songs was performed by <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>, who would be the subject of a <a href="/wiki/Tupac:_Resurrection" title="Tupac: Resurrection">documentary</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Tupac:_Resurrection_(soundtrack)" title="Tupac: Resurrection (soundtrack)">soundtrack produced by Eminem</a>, who also produced a <a href="/wiki/Loyal_to_the_Game" title="Loyal to the Game">posthumous album by Tupac</a>. The album also features four songs by the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> and its members, and two songs by <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, who eventually signed to <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a>, headed by Shady Records artist <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>. Both albums were also made available in censored versions, removing most of the strong profanity and violent content.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charts">Charts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_from_and_Inspired_by_the_Motion_Picture_8_Mile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Charts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/U.S." title="U.S." class="mw-redirect">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> Albums Chart that year with over 702,000 copies sold and 507,000 sold in the second week also finishing the year as the fifth best-selling album of 2002 with US sales of 3.4 million, despite only two months of release. As of March 2012, it has sold more than 4,854,000 albums.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It debuted at #1 on the Canadian Albums Chart with sales of 44,500 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It also reached #1 on the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> Compilations Chart <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/ARIAnet" title="ARIAnet" class="mw-redirect">ARIAnet</a> Albums Chart. It featured Eminem's worldwide chart-topping single, "<a href="/wiki/Lose_Yourself" title="Lose Yourself">Lose Yourself</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content_and_censorship">Content and censorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_from_and_Inspired_by_the_Motion_Picture_8_Mile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Content and censorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The clean version of the <i>8 Mile</i> soundtrack removes most of the profanity and violent content. The only word left uncensored on the soundtrack, is the word "ass" (except on "Places to Go" by 50 Cent, where the word "ass" is used twice, but the word was only censored once). No other words (e.g., "bitch", "shit" and "goddamn") are uncensored on the clean version of "8 Mile". "That's My Nigga Fo' Real", by rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Zee" title="Young Zee" class="mw-redirect">Young Zee</a>, is listed as "That's My ***** Fo' Real" on the clean version. In "Rap Game" by <a href="/wiki/D12" title="D12">D12</a> featuring 50 Cent in <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a>'s verse, the word "shit" is uncensored (even on the clean version), and in Eminem's verse, the words "<a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Dick_Cheney" title="Dick Cheney">Cheney</a>" are censored on both edited and explicit versions. In "Rabbit Run" by Eminem, the word "fuck" was uncensored in the clean version of the soundtrack.</p>
