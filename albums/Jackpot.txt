<p><i><b>Jackpot</b></i> is the first album by the rapper <a href="/wiki/Chingy" title="Chingy">Chingy</a>. Sales of the album achieved <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">Triple Platinum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV928_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV928-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It was released on July 15, 2003, by the <a href="/wiki/Disturbing_tha_Peace" title="Disturbing tha Peace">Disturbing tha Peace</a> label. Anchored by the smash single "<a href="/wiki/Right_Thurr" title="Right Thurr">Right Thurr</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-MTV928_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV928-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> this album also had two other top three hits, "<a href="/wiki/Holidae_In" title="Holidae In">Holidae In</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/One_Call_Away" title="One Call Away">One Call Away</a>" featuring J-Weav. The album was produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Trak_Starz" title="The Trak Starz">The Trak Starz</a> except for "Bagg Up", which was produced by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine ranked <i>Jackpot</i> at number 151 on the magazine's Top 200 Albums of the Decade.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
