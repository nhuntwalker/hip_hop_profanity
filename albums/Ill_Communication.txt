<p><i><b>Ill Communication</b></i> is the fourth <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>. It was released on May 23, 1994<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> via <a href="/wiki/Grand_Royal" title="Grand Royal">Grand Royal Records</a>.</p>
<p>It was their second number one album on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>. The album was supported by the single "<a href="/wiki/Sabotage_(song)" title="Sabotage (song)">Sabotage</a>", which was accompanied by a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> (directed by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Jonze" title="Spike Jonze">Spike Jonze</a>) that parodied 1970s cop shows. The album was also their second <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">triple platinum</a> album.</p>
<p><i>Ill Communication</i> was co-produced by Beastie Boys and <a href="/wiki/Mario_C" title="Mario C" class="mw-redirect">Mario C</a>. Featuring musical contributions from <a href="/wiki/Money_Mark" title="Money Mark">Money Mark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bobo" title="Eric Bobo" class="mw-redirect">Eric Bobo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amery_Smith" title="Amery Smith">Amery "AWOL" Smith</a>, vocal contributions from <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(rapper)" title="Q-Tip (rapper)" class="mw-redirect">Q-Tip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>.</p>
<p>Mike D and Adam Yauch collaborated with Gibran Evans of T.A.Z. to create the album packaging, and to choose the unique cover photo taken by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Davidson_(photographer)" title="Bruce Davidson (photographer)">Bruce Davidson</a>. The hand-drawn typeface was created by designer <a href="/wiki/Jim_Evans_(artist)" title="Jim Evans (artist)">Jim Evans</a> specifically for <i>Ill Communication</i>, and was used throughout the promotion of the album. The album booklet also features the artwork "Gaia" by <a href="/wiki/Alex_Grey" title="Alex Grey">Alex Grey</a>.</p>
