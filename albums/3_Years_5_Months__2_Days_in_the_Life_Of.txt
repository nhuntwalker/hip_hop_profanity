<p><i><b>3 Years, 5 Months &amp; 2 Days in the Life Of...</b></i> is the debut album by American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Arrested_Development_(hip_hop_group)" title="Arrested Development (hip hop group)" class="mw-redirect">Arrested Development</a>, released in 1992 (see <a href="/wiki/1992_in_music" title="1992 in music">1992 in music</a>). The album's chart success was the beginning of the popularization of <a href="/wiki/Southern_hip_hop" title="Southern hip hop">Southern hip hop</a>. <i>3 Years, 5 Months &amp; 2 Days in the Life Of...</i> stood in stark contrast to the <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a> that ruled the hip hop charts in 1992 (such as <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>), in its focus on <a href="/wiki/Spirituality" title="Spirituality">spirituality</a>, peace and love. It was voted as the best album of the year in <a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a> <a href="/wiki/Pazz_%26_Jop" title="Pazz &amp; Jop">Pazz &amp; Jop</a> critics poll. The album's title refers to the length of time it took Arrested Development to get a record contract.</p>
<p>The song "Tennessee" is part of the <a href="/wiki/The_Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame%27s_500_Songs_that_Shaped_Rock_and_Roll" title="The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's 500 Songs that Shaped Rock and Roll" class="mw-redirect">The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's 500 Songs that Shaped Rock and Roll</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
