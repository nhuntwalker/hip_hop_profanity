				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="hatnote">"Curtiss" redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Curtis_(disambiguation)" title="Curtis (disambiguation)">Curtis (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Curtis</b> or <b>Curtiss</b> is a common English <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of Anglo-Norman origin derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>curteis</i> (Modern French <i>courtois</i>, surname Courtois<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>) which means "polite, courteous, or well-bred".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It is a compound of <i>curt-</i> ″court″ and <i>-eis</i> ″-ish″.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The spelling <i>u</i> to render [u] in Old French was mainly <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Norman" title="Anglo-Norman">Anglo-Norman</a> and Norman, when the spelling <i>o</i> [u] was the usual Parisian French one, Modern French <i>ou</i> [u]. <i>-eis</i> is the Old French suffix for <i>-ois</i>, Western French (including Anglo-Norman) keeps <i>-eis</i>, simplified <i>-is</i> in English. The word <i>court</i> shares the same etymology but retains a Modern French spelling, after the orthography had changed.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It was brought to England (and subsequently, the rest of the Isles) via the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" title="Norman Conquest" class="mw-redirect">Norman Conquest</a>.</p>
<p>Many Hungarian immigrants in English speaking countries with the last name <i>Kertész</i> ″Gardener″ have adopted the name <i>Curtis</i>, since it is pronounced similarly and helped them integrate into their new community.</p>
