<p><i><b>The Red Light District</b></i> is the fifth <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> from <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Records" title="Def Jam Records" class="mw-redirect">Def Jam</a> recording artist <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The album was released on December 7, 2004 and was eventually certified double platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a>. It debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> album chart with 322,000 copies sold in its first week. The album's title refers to a district where <a href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">prostitution</a> is common; <a href="/wiki/Red-light_district" title="Red-light district">red-light district</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> This was the last album that saw Ludacris wearing his once signature <a href="/wiki/Cornrows" title="Cornrows">cornrows</a>. The album has sold over 2.1 million copies to date.</p>
