<p><i><b>It's On (<del>Dr. Dre</del>) 187<sup>um</sup> Killa</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> by <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a> released in <a href="/wiki/1993_in_music" title="1993 in music">1993</a>. Eazy-E's 2nd EP is a response to <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>. The EP was certified 2x Multi-Platinum in 1994 by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It is Eazy's most successful release, peaking on #5 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> and #1 on <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>, while selling 110,600 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> This is the last completed album Eazy made before his death.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Album_information">Album information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=It%27s_On_(Dr._Dre)_187um_Killa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Album information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EP contained the <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> diss track "<a href="/wiki/Real_Muthaphuckkin_G%27s" title="Real Muthaphuckkin G's">Real Muthaphuckkin G's</a>". Eazy-E released music videos for "<a href="/wiki/Any_Last_Werdz" title="Any Last Werdz">Any Last Werdz</a>" and "Real Muthaphuckkin G's". It went multi-platinum and became one of Eazy-E's most popular recordings. Eazy-E was originally intending to release a follow up to <i><a href="/wiki/Eazy-Duz-It" title="Eazy-Duz-It">Eazy-Duz-It</a></i> named <i>Temporary Insanity</i>, which was going to be released as a double album.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> However, after the several insults Dr. Dre made in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>, Eazy-E decided to release this EP. Dr. Dre is mentioned on every track except "Gimmie That Nutt" and "Boyz N Tha Hood (G-Mix)".</p>
