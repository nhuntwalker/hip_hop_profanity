<p><i><b>400 Degreez</b></i> is the third <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_(rapper)" title="Juvenile (rapper)">Juvenile</a>, released November 3, 1998,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> on <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>. The album received rave reviews and introduced Juvenile to mainstream hip hop listeners, selling over four million copies in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It remains Juvenile's best-selling album of his solo career and the best-selling album ever released on <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>. The album was certified <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">4x Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>, on December 19, 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Ha_(song)" title="Ha (song)">Ha</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Back_That_Azz_Up" title="Back That Azz Up" class="mw-redirect">Back That Azz Up</a>" became <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> hits in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. "Back That Azz Up" peaked at #19 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> along with "Ha" which peaked at #68 on the hot 100. "Follow Me Now" was also a single, but did not catch on as the other two did. The album peaked at #2 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Charts" title="Billboard Music Charts" class="mw-redirect">Billboard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip_Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums" class="mw-redirect">Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums</a> and at #9 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> in 1999. It also went to claim the number-one position on the Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums chart on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Year-End" title="Billboard Year-End">Billboard Year-End</a> chart for 1999. As a single, "Back That Azz Up" was released, credited, and charted as the more censored "Back That Thang Up". Also the album features a bonus remix of the single "Ha" with New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, the only guest appearance outside of Cash Money. As of 2013, 400 Degreez has sold over 5 million copies worldwide.</p>
