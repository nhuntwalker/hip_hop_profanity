<p><i><b>Curtain Call: The Hits</b></i> is the first greatest hits <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a> released by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> by <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>. It was released on December 6, 2005, under <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a>. The album collects Eminem's most popular songs, as well as four new tracks, including a live version of "<a href="/wiki/Stan_(song)" title="Stan (song)">Stan</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> from the <a href="/wiki/43rd_Grammy_Awards" title="43rd Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">43rd Grammy Awards</a>, plus new songs "Fack", "<a href="/wiki/When_I%27m_Gone_(Eminem_song)" title="When I'm Gone (Eminem song)">When I'm Gone</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shake_That" title="Shake That">Shake That</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>.</p>
<p>The album was certified double-platinum in the US, triple-platinum in Australia and the UK, and quadruple-platinum in New Zealand. It reached #1 on several charts, including the UK and US Albums Chart. <i>Curtain Call: The Hits</i> was released nine years after the rerelease of Eminem's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Infinite_(Eminem_album)" title="Infinite (Eminem album)">Infinite</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtain_Call:_The_Hits&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Curtain Call: The Hits</i> debuted at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>, after two sales days, in a similar fashion to Eminem's previous album <i><a href="/wiki/Encore_(Eminem_album)" title="Encore (Eminem album)">Encore</a></i>. The album racked up first-week sales of nearly 441,000 and with close to 324,000 scans the second week for a two-week stay at number one. It slipped from no. 1 to no. 4 in its third week but surged 33 percent to finish with sales close to 430,000. The disc scored nearly 1.2 million scans in its first three weeks of release. It also gave Eminem his fifth straight number 1 album in the US and UK when including the <a href="/wiki/8_Mile_(soundtrack)" title="8 Mile (soundtrack)" class="mw-redirect"><i>8 Mile</i> soundtrack</a>. <i>Curtain Call</i> was certified double-platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> As of November 2013, the record has sold 3,782,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album's two singles, "<a href="/wiki/When_I%27m_Gone_(Eminem_song)" title="When I'm Gone (Eminem song)">When I'm Gone</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shake_That" title="Shake That">Shake That</a>", peaked at numbers 8 and 6 respectively in the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. Only "When I'm Gone" qualified for the charts in the UK, where it peaked at #4.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clean_version">Clean version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtain_Call:_The_Hits&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Clean version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A censored version of the album is also available. It features 15 tracks instead of 17 with "Intro" and "Fack" removed and "<a href="/wiki/My_Name_Is" title="My Name Is">My Name Is</a>" put to track one. The tracks appear exactly how they appeared on the clean versions of their respective albums except for the song "<a href="/wiki/Guilty_Conscience" title="Guilty Conscience">Guilty Conscience</a>", which adds a chorus. Certain profanities remain on several tracks, as words including "shit", "bitch", and "ass" were not censored on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Slim_Shady_LP" title="The Slim Shady LP">The Slim Shady LP</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP" title="The Marshall Mathers LP">The Marshall Mathers LP</a></i>. However, on "Just Lose It", the clean version leaves "ass" uncensored, while on the album <i>Encore</i>, the word "ass" was replaced with "thing".</p>
