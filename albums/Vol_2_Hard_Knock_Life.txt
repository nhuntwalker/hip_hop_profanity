<p><i><b>Vol. 2... Hard Knock Life</b></i> is the third <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, released on September 29, 1998, on <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The album propelled Jay-Z into superstar status and cemented him as a household name in the post <a href="/wiki/Biggie" title="Biggie" class="mw-redirect">Biggie</a>/<a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> era of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a>. It is Jay-Z's best selling album as of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vol._2..._Hard_Knock_Life&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> and was certified 5x Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a> in 2000 and won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Grammy Award for Best Rap Album</a> in 1999. The album debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200, selling over 350,000 copies in its first week,<sup id="cite_ref-reiss_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reiss-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and also went to #1 on the Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart, marking the first of nine consecutive chart-topping albums on both charts to date from Jay-Z, excluding collaboration albums. Upon its release, the album received generally mixed to positive reviews from most music critics, while hip hop purists felt his aims to be a crossover success had compromised the quality and complexity of his music.</p>
<p>In the liner notes of the album, Jay-Z gives his thoughts on various tracks. The lyrics to the fast paced "<a href="/wiki/Nigga_What,_Nigga_Who_(Originator_99)" title="Nigga What, Nigga Who (Originator 99)">Nigga What, Nigga Who (Originator 99)</a>" are also included.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vol._2..._Hard_Knock_Life&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Speaking in December 1998 to noted UK urban writer Pete Lewis of the award-winning <i><a href="/wiki/Blues_%26_Soul" title="Blues &amp; Soul">Blues &amp; Soul</a></i>, Jay described the background to the album's lyrical themes: "Primarily I see myself as so much more than a rapper. I really believe I'm the voice for a lot of people who don't have that microphone or who can't rap. So I wanted to represent and tell the story of everybody who's been through what I've been through, or knows somebody that has. I also wanted to speak about our lifestyle to people who - though they may live, say, in the suburbs and not be part of that world - still want to know about it and understand it."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vol._2..._Hard_Knock_Life&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several tracks on this album feature a rougher sound than the glossier <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy</a> production on <i><a href="/wiki/In_My_Lifetime,_Vol._1" title="In My Lifetime, Vol. 1">In My Lifetime, Vol. 1</a></i>, most notably the three tracks produced by <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" title="Ruff Ryders" class="mw-redirect">Ruff Ryders</a> beatmaker <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>. With the exception of Stevie J on "Ride or Die," Bad Boy producers play no role in <i>Vol. 2</i>, though Jay-Z enlisted <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a> for a more pop-oriented sound on three of the albums' singles. This would also be Jay-Z's last album to feature his mentor <a href="/wiki/Jaz-O" title="Jaz-O">Big Jaz</a>.</p>
