<p><i><b>Me Against the World</b></i> is the third <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>. It was released March 14, 1995 on the <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> label. The album was composed of un-used tracks from the Thug Life era, and from other studio sessions from 1993 to 1994. It was his impending prison sentence, troubles with the police and Shakur being poor, which many believe might have contributed to Shakur's artistic re-emergence on record, as his material is believed by Steve Huey of <i><a href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic" class="mw-redirect">Allmusic</a></i> to have become markedly more "confessional", "reflective", and "soul-baring".<sup id="cite_ref-AllmusOvrvw_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllmusOvrvw-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><i>Me Against the World</i>, released while Shakur was imprisoned, made an immediate impact on the charts, debuting at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. This made Shakur the first artist to have an album debut at number one on <i>Billboard</i> 200 while serving time in prison. The album served as one of Shakur's most positively reviewed albums, with many calling it the magnum opus of his career, and is considered one of the greatest and most influential hip hop albums of all-time.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Me Against the World</i> won best rap album at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Soul_Train_Music_Awards" title="1996 Soul Train Music Awards">1996 Soul Train Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> It achieved double platinum status and has sold 3,524,567 copies in the United States as of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Me_Against_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1992, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> was already a success in the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> industry, with two <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification#Singles" title="RIAA certification">gold-certified</a> singles that reached the top twenty on the pop charts ("<a href="/wiki/I_Get_Around_(2Pac_song)" title="I Get Around (2Pac song)" class="mw-redirect">I Get Around</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Keep_Ya_Head_Up" title="Keep Ya Head Up">Keep Ya Head Up</a>"), and a <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification#Albums" title="RIAA certification">platinum-selling</a> <a href="/wiki/Sophomore" title="Sophomore">sophomore</a> album that would peak just inside the top twenty-five of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_4_My_N.I.G.G.A.Z." title="Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z." class="mw-redirect">Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z.</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-2PacRIAAStats_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2PacRIAAStats-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the 22-year-old artist had a series of incidents and charges of breaking the law. In the summer of 1993, Shakur was charged for assaulting director <a href="/wiki/Hughes_brothers" title="Hughes brothers">Allen Hughes</a> while filming <i><a href="/wiki/Menace_II_Society" title="Menace II Society">Menace II Society</a></i>; Shakur was later sentenced to fifteen days in jail. Later, in October 1993, Shakur was charged with shooting two off-duty police officers in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, though the charges would eventually be dismissed. In November, Shakur and two members of his entourage were charged with sexually assaulting a female fan, for which they were found guilty and sentenced to 4.5 years in jail.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> According to Shakur, the album was made to show the hip hop audience his respect for the art form. Lyrically, Shakur intentionally tried to make the album more personal and reflective than his previous efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-TRBook-166_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TRBook-166-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recording_and_production">Recording and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Me_Against_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recording and production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The musical production on the album was considered by several music critics to be the best on any of <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Shakur</a>'s albums up to that point in his career. Steve "Flash" Juon at RapReviews gave the production on the album a perfect 10 of 10 rating, particularly praising tracks like "<a href="/wiki/So_Many_Tears" title="So Many Tears">So Many Tears</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Temptations_(song)" title="Temptations (song)">Temptations</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-RpRvwsBTTL_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RpRvwsBTTL-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jon_Pareles" title="Jon Pareles">Jon Pareles</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">New York Times</a></i> remarked that the production had a "fatalistic calm, in a commercial mold". He compared the album's production and synthesized hooks to that of <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <a href="/wiki/G-funk" title="G-funk">G-funk</a> style, stating that "while 2Pac doesn't sing, other voices do, providing smooth melody".<sup id="cite_ref-NYTReview_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTReview-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> James Bernard at <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> was not quite as enthusiastic about the album's production, remarking that Shakur's "vocals are buried deep in the mix. That's a shame—if they were more in-your-face, the lackluster beats might be less noticeable." <sup id="cite_ref-EWReview_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EWReview-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The album's recording sessions took place at ten different studios, while it was mastered at <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Grundman_Mastering" title="Bernie Grundman Mastering" class="mw-redirect">Bernie Grundman Mastering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Although the album was originally released on Interscope. <a href="/wiki/Amaru_Entertainment" title="Amaru Entertainment">Amaru Entertainment</a>, the label owned by Shakur's mother <a href="/wiki/Afeni_Shakur" title="Afeni Shakur">Afeni Shakur</a>, has since released the album twice.<sup id="cite_ref-full_discogs_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-full_discogs-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Me_Against_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>It was like a <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a> record. It was down-home. It was all my fears, all the things I just couldn't sleep about. Everybody thought I was living so well and doing so good that I wanted to explain it. And it took a whole album to get it all out. I get to tell my innermost, darkest secrets I tell my own personal problems.<sup id="cite_ref-TRBook-166_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TRBook-166-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="templatequotecite">——Tupac Shakur</div>
</blockquote>
<p>Some of the album's main themes concern the loss of innocence, <a href="/wiki/Paranoia" title="Paranoia">paranoia</a>, and occasional <a href="/wiki/Self-loathing" title="Self-loathing" class="mw-redirect">self-loathing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AllmusOvrvw_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllmusOvrvw-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Much attention is paid to subjects such as the pain of urban survival.<sup id="cite_ref-RpRvwsBTTL_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RpRvwsBTTL-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Not all of the music deals with such extremely bleak subject matter, however. Some tracks, such as "Old School", lean more to the nostalgic, though somewhat bittersweet side in Shakur's remembrance of his youth and the early days of hip hop music.<sup id="cite_ref-AllmusOvrvw_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllmusOvrvw-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RpRvwsBTTL_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RpRvwsBTTL-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The album is also well known for the more sensitive tracks "<a href="/wiki/Dear_Mama" title="Dear Mama">Dear Mama</a>" and "Can U Get Away", which are both directed towards and reveal Shakur's devotion to the women he loves. On "Dear Mama", Shakur pays tribute to and expresses his undying affection for his own mother, continuously reminding her that though his actions might sometimes seem to state otherwise, "you are appreciated".<sup id="cite_ref-RpRvwsBTTL_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RpRvwsBTTL-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> On the track "Can U Get Away", Shakur attempts to impress a <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lopes" title="Lisa Lopes">woman</a> who has managed to gain his affections, away from the woman's <a href="/wiki/Domestic_violence" title="Domestic violence">abusive relationship</a>. Four of the most eerie and revered tracks on the album are "If I Die 2Nite", "Lord Knows" "Outlaw" which directly references the shooting that Tupac went through before it happened, and "Fuck The World" Throughout the entirety of the album Shakur employs various poetical deliveries, ranging from <a href="/wiki/Alliteration" title="Alliteration">alliteration</a> ("If I Die 2Nite"), to the use of paired <a href="/wiki/Couplet" title="Couplet">couplets</a> ("Lord Knows").<sup id="cite_ref-RpRvwsBTTL_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RpRvwsBTTL-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Me_Against_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Dear_Mama" title="Dear Mama">Dear Mama</a>" was released as the album's first single in February 1995, along with the track "Old School" as the <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">B-side</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> "Dear Mama" would be the album's most successful single, topping the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Tracks" title="Hot Rap Tracks" class="mw-redirect">Hot Rap Singles</a> chart, and peaking at the ninth spot on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BllbrdSnglsAllmsc_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BllbrdSnglsAllmsc-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> The single was certified platinum in July 1995,<sup id="cite_ref-2PacRIAAStats_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2PacRIAAStats-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> and later placed at number 51 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Year-End_Hot_100_singles_of_1995" title="Billboard Year-End Hot 100 singles of 1995">year-end charts</a>.</p>
<p>The second single, "<a href="/wiki/So_Many_Tears" title="So Many Tears">So Many Tears</a>", was released in June, four months after the first single.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> The single would reach the number six spot on the Hot Rap Singles chart, and the 44th on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-BllbrdSnglsAllmsc_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BllbrdSnglsAllmsc-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Temptations_(song)" title="Temptations (song)">Temptations</a>", released in August, was the third and final single from the album.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> The single would be the least successful of the three released, but still did fairly well on the charts, reaching number 68 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, 35 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Singles &amp; Tracks</a>, and 13 on the Hot Rap Singles charts.<sup id="cite_ref-BllbrdSnglsAllmsc_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BllbrdSnglsAllmsc-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>In a contemporary review, Cheo H. Coker at <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> called the album Shakur's best and said it was "by and large a work of pain, anger and burning desperation — [it] is the first time 2Pac has taken the conflicting forces tugging at his psyche head-on".<sup id="cite_ref-RSReview_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RSReview-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jon_Pareles" title="Jon Pareles">Jon Pareles</a>, writing in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, called Shakur the "<a href="/wiki/St._Augustine_of_Hippo" title="St. Augustine of Hippo" class="mw-redirect">St. Augustine</a> of gangster rap" due to his ambivalence towards the behavior and nature of the gangster lifestyle.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTReview_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTReview-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> In a negative review, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i> felt that Shakur is "witless" when dealing with fundamental hip hop themes of persecution and accused him of "self-pity", saying that "the subtext of his persecution complex is his self-regard".<sup id="cite_ref-Christgau_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christgau-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In a retrospective review, <a href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic" class="mw-redirect">Allmusic</a> editor Steve Huey dubbed the album "[Shakur's] most thematically consistent, least self-contradicting work", and stated, "it may not be his definitive album, but it just might be his best".<sup id="cite_ref-AllmusOvrvw_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllmusOvrvw-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Steve "Flash" Juon of RapReview seemed to feel differently, remarking that the album "is not only the quintessential Shakur album, but one of the most important rap albums released in the 1990s as a whole".<sup id="cite_ref-RpRvwsBTTL_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RpRvwsBTTL-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Me_Against_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/38th_Grammy_Awards" title="38th Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">38th Grammy Awards</a>, in 1996, <i>Me Against the World</i> was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a> and the single "Dear Mama" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance">Best Rap Solo Performance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2008, the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Recording_Merchandisers" title="National Association of Recording Merchandisers" class="mw-redirect">National Association of Recording Merchandisers</a>, in conjunction with the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a>, recognized <i>Me Against the World</i> as one of the "most influential and popular albums", ranking it number 170 on a list of 200 other albums by artists of various musical genres.<sup id="cite_ref-RH200_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RH200-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Me_Against_the_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at the number one spot on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart with 240,000 copies sold in the first week, and became certified double platinum by the end of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-BllbrdAlbms_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BllbrdAlbms-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> Likewise, it also debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart, thus giving 2Pac the first number one album on both R&amp;B and Pop charts.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> While Shakur was in prison, the album over-took <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Bruce_Springsteen_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Bruce Springsteen album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> as the best-selling album in the United States, a feat which he took pride in.<sup id="cite_ref-TRBook-166_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TRBook-166-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Shakur became the first artist to have a number one album while serving a prison sentence.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As of 2013, the album has been certified gold in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
