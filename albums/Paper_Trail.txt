<p><i><b>Paper Trail</b></i> is the sixth <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, released September 30, 2008, on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Hustle_Records" title="Grand Hustle Records">Grand Hustle Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>. He began to write songs for the album as he awaited trial for federal weapons and possession charges. Unlike his past albums, he wrote his lyrics down on paper, which he had not done since his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Serious" title="I'm Serious">I'm Serious</a></i> (2001).</p>
<p>The album debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 568,000 copies in its first week, becoming T.I.'s highest debut sales to date, and the fourth highest debut of the year. The album was eventually certified double <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) on August 27, 2009. It received generally favorable reviews from critics, and is T.I.'s most successful album to date.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The album spawned eight singles, four of which reached the top five of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, with two singles peaking at number one.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_recording">Background and recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paper_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 13, 2007, while awaiting trial for federal weapons charges, T.I. announced he had been writing songs for a new album to be entitled <i>Paper Trail</i>. He wrote his lyrics down on paper, which he had not done since his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Serious" title="I'm Serious">I'm Serious</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The album highlights his "fear, anger and guilt" as he awaited trial.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>T.I. recorded nearly 100 tracks during the production for <i>Paper Trail</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> including a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a> on a track entitled "Out in the Cold".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> However the song was left out of the final cut, but T.I. told <i><a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a></i> that he may add some of the cut tracks to his next album.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album was scheduled to be released in September 2008, but was subsequently moved up to an August 12 after the release of the promotional and lead single "<a href="/wiki/No_Matter_What_(T.I._song)" title="No Matter What (T.I. song)">No Matter What</a>" and high demand of the album. At the time, T.I. said that the first single may have been "Top of the World", originally featuring <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, explaining that the song was "a reflective song about our humble beginnings and how far we've come".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The album was pushed back for a release date of September 2, then September 9 and to its final release date of September 30, 2008, in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The album was first released on September 29, 2008, in continental Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-hungmedien_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungmedien-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> released from the album was a song titled "<a href="/wiki/No_Matter_What_(T.I._song)" title="No Matter What (T.I. song)">No Matter What</a>". It was posted on <a href="/wiki/StreetCred" title="StreetCred">StreetCred</a>'s website on April 29, 2008, as a <a href="/wiki/Promotional_recording" title="Promotional recording">promotional recording</a>, then released as a single on May 6, along with a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>, which he premiered on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <a href="/wiki/FNMTV" title="FNMTV">FNMTV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The song narrates T.I. rising above his misfortunes, including a prison sentence for weapon charges.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On August 19, 2008, the album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> "<a href="/wiki/Whatever_You_Like_(T.I._song)" title="Whatever You Like (T.I. song)">Whatever You Like</a>", was made available for digital download.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The song reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> on September 6, 2008 and set a new record for biggest one-week jump to the top position, going from number seventy-one to number one, becoming his first number one song on the chart as a lead artist and best opening-week sales by a rap track since <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a> began compiling download data in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> T.I. announced that he was "ecstatic" and "overjoyed" after setting a record for the biggest jump to top of the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After the success of "Whatever You Like", <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes</a> published the release dates for the upcoming singles, "<a href="/wiki/Swing_Ya_Rag" title="Swing Ya Rag">Swing Ya Rag</a>" on August 26, "<a href="/wiki/What_Up,_What%27s_Haapnin%27" title="What Up, What's Haapnin'">What Up, What's Haapnin'</a>" on September 2 and "Ready for Whatever" on September 23, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Swagga_Like_Us" title="Swagga Like Us" class="mw-redirect">Swagga Like Us</a>", a song with T.I., <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> - which samples "<a href="/wiki/Paper_Planes_(song)" title="Paper Planes (song)" class="mw-redirect">Paper Planes</a>" by <a href="/wiki/M.I.A._(artist)" title="M.I.A. (artist)" class="mw-redirect">M.I.A.</a> - was then released as a single for <i>Paper Trail</i> and debuted in the Hot 100 at number five.<sup id="cite_ref-slu-mtv_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slu-mtv-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album's seventh single, "<a href="/wiki/Live_Your_Life" title="Live Your Life">Live Your Life</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, broke the record for biggest leap to number one, when it jumped from number eighty to number one on the Hot 100, giving T.I. his second Hot 100 number one as a lead artist, and Rihanna her fifth overall. The song also set a first week digital record, when it sold 334,000 digital downloads in its first week of availability, a record that was previously held by <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Touch_My_Body" title="Touch My Body">Touch My Body</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The eighth single was confirmed to be "<a href="/wiki/Dead_and_Gone" title="Dead and Gone">Dead and Gone</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, by the song's co-producer Rob Knox.<sup id="cite_ref-robknoxhiphopdx_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robknoxhiphopdx-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> T.I. himself later confirmed the release of the single.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> The song was charted by <i>Billboard</i> before the announcement of the single. The song reached number two on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 on February 27, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Overall, eight songs from the album charted on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 including the four main singles, "56 Bars (Intro)", "I'm Illy", "Ready for Whatever", and "My Life Your Entertainment" despite not being singles. The song "56 Bars (Intro)" features T.I. freestyling 56 straight bars. A music video was released for the song "Slide Show" which highlighted a photo collage that followed T.I.'s career as a recording artist.</p>
<p>On August 22, 2008, the songs "Like I Do" featuring <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>, "Let My Beat Pound", "<a href="/wiki/My_Life_Your_Entertainment" title="My Life Your Entertainment" class="mw-redirect">My Life Your Entertainment</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Swagga_Like_Us" title="Swagga Like Us" class="mw-redirect">Swagga Like Us</a>" were all <a href="/wiki/Internet_leak" title="Internet leak">leaked online</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-slu-mtv_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slu-mtv-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> The track "Every Chance I Get" was featured on the promotional trailer for <i><a href="/wiki/Armored_(film)" title="Armored (film)">Armored</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Short" title="Columbus Short">Columbus Short</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paper_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paper_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a>, <i>Paper Trail</i> sold 357,000 copies in the United States in its first three days<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> and went on to debut at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with 568,000 copies sold. It is T.I.'s best debut to date and the fourth highest debut of 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> In its second week, the album remained at number one with sales of 177,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> In its third week, the album fell to number two selling 131,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> In its fourth week, the album fell to number three selling 93,427 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> In its fifth week, the album fell to number seven selling 76,560 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> It ultimately spent 55 weeks on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup id="cite_ref-Paper_Trail_-_T.I._25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paper_Trail_-_T.I.-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album has sold over two million copies in the United States and has been certified <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">double platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-certification_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-certification-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p><i>Paper Trail</i> received generally positive reviews from music critics. At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean" class="mw-redirect">average</a> score of 74, based on 19 reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> Several critics praised the production on the album; Andy Kellman of <a href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic" class="mw-redirect">Allmusic</a> noted that <a href="/wiki/DJ_Toomp" title="DJ Toomp">DJ Toomp</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Danja_(musician)" title="Danja (musician)" class="mw-redirect">Danja</a>'s production appearance provided "some much needed punch", which placed the album above T.I.'s previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/T.I._vs._T.I.P." title="T.I. vs. T.I.P.">T.I. vs. T.I.P.</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Margeaux Watson of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> said that the "outstanding production" proved that T.I. "still knows how to have a good time".<sup id="cite_ref-EW_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> Jonah Weiner of <i><a href="/wiki/Blender_(magazine)" title="Blender (magazine)">Blender</a></i> wrote that "T.I. boats one of gangsta rap's most mellifluous voices and more polysyllabic lexicons, and when he combines the two, he's dazzling, hypnotic, virtuosic."<sup id="cite_ref-Weiner_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiner-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> Michael Saba of <i><a href="/wiki/Paste_(magazine)" title="Paste (magazine)">Paste</a></i> said that, "Even with the burden of sloppy crossover tracks, <i>Paper Trail</i> has enough standout moments for T.I.’s throne to remain secure for now."<sup id="cite_ref-Paste_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paste-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/MSN_Music" title="MSN Music">MSN Music</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> called <i>Paper Trail</i> an "expediently excessive piece of rich-get-richer" and gave it an "A–",<sup id="cite_ref-Christgau_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christgau-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> indicating "the kind of garden-variety good record that is the great luxury of musical micromarketing and overproduction. Anyone open to its aesthetic will enjoy more than half its tracks".<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Wilson McBee of <a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a> praised the Toomp-produced songs and called the sound "ecstasies of sweltering synth lines, ground-shaking 808 patterns and breathless verbalizing".<sup id="cite_ref-Slant_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slant-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> However, McBee felt that the songs that were directly about T.I.'s prison sentence for weapons charges ("<a href="/wiki/No_Matter_What_(T.I._song)" title="No Matter What (T.I. song)">No Matter What</a>", "Ready for Whatever") were among the weakest on the album, saying that "admitting guilt, making excuses and expressing no regrets falls flat".<sup id="cite_ref-Slant_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slant-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Sharing a similar sentiment, <a href="/wiki/Jody_Rosen" title="Jody Rosen">Jody Rosen</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> called the songs "mostly dispenses with the <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac</a>-wannabe gangsta-confessor pretensions to deliver catchy, tight, bombastic pop-rap alongside a who's-who of megastar guests", saying that T.I. was a "well-oiled hit machine who's more fun than deep".<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paper_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The album earned T.I. several award nominations and wins, including a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> and winning a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Swagga_Like_Us" title="Swagga Like Us" class="mw-redirect">Swagga Like Us</a>" at the <a href="/wiki/51st_Grammy_Awards" title="51st Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">51st Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Paper Trail</i> won 'CD of the Year' at the <a href="/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards#2009_winners" title="BET Hip Hop Awards">2009 BET Hip Hop Awards</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> and "<a href="/wiki/Dead_and_Gone" title="Dead and Gone">Dead and Gone</a>" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap/Sung_Collaboration" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration">Best Rap/Sung Collaboration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Best Rap Song</a> at the <a href="/wiki/52nd_Grammy_Awards" title="52nd Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">52nd Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-release">Re-release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paper_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Re-release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The re-release issue of <i>Paper Trail</i> was scheduled to be released on August 18, 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> and would contain five new songs.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> The release was tentatively titled <i>Paper Trail: Case Closed</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-videoshoot_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videoshoot-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> T.I. stated that "Like I Do" featuring <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a> and "My Dear" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ciara" title="Ciara">Ciara</a> were two possible tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> Rob Knox, the co-producer of "<a href="/wiki/Dead_and_Gone" title="Dead and Gone">Dead and Gone</a>", was first to announce the re-release. Knox also said that the first single off the re-release would be another song produced by himself and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-robknoxhiphopdx_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robknoxhiphopdx-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The first single was released on May 14, 2009 as "Don't Forget" which featured a chorus sung by <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>. The final version was released on May 26, 2009, titled "<a href="/wiki/Remember_Me_(T.I._song)" title="Remember Me (T.I. song)">Remember Me</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> A video was filmed before his prison sentence by <a href="/wiki/Erik_White" title="Erik White">Erik White</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> T.I. stated two other possible song choices including the collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a> entitled "Out in the Cold" and the original version of "On Top of the World" with <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> A video for "<a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Life" title="Hell of a Life">Hell of a Life</a>" was shot and was to be featured on the re-release.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> Another song titled "The Way You Want It" was confirmed to be added to the re-release.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> The final version of "Hell of a Life" was released on October 1, 2009, an accompanying music video was released on October 5, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> The music video reflects T.I.'s career and narrates the final 24 hours before beginning his sentence for illegal weapons.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On July 14, 2009, a spokesperson for <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> told <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> that the re-release was shelved indefinitely and the additional songs will be released digitally.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> In August 2009, an EP was released titled <i>Paper Trail: Case Closed EP</i> and featured original <i>Paper Trail</i> singles, "Remember Me" and "Hell of a Life".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
