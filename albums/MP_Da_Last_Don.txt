<p><i><b>MP da Last Don</b></i> is the seventh <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album" class="mw-redirect">studio album</a> by American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper" class="mw-redirect">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a> on <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a>. It debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Top_200" title="Billboard Top 200" class="mw-redirect">Billboard Top 200</a> charts selling 495,000 copies in the first week. It gained mixed reviews. It was also released about the same time as the straight-to-video short film, <i><a href="/wiki/MP_Da_Last_Don_(film)" title="MP Da Last Don (film)" class="mw-redirect">MP Da Last Don</a></i>. The album was certified 4x Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA" class="mw-redirect">RIAA</a>, with over four million copies sold, making it Master P's best selling album of his career. The track entitled "Ghetto Love" on disc 2 samples <a href="/wiki/Jaco_Pastorius" title="Jaco Pastorius">Jaco Pastorius</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Portrait_of_Tracy" title="Portrait of Tracy">Portrait of Tracy</a>".</p>
